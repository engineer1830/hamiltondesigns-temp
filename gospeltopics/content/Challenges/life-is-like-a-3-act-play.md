# Life Is Like a 3‑Act Play

President Boyd K. Packer likened life to a grand three‑act play.  
The key takeaway is simple but profound:

> **Mortality is Act II — and that is not where “happily ever after” happens.**

## The Three Acts

President Packer described the Plan of Redemption as a drama in three acts:

1. **Act I — The Premortal Life (The First Estate)**  
2. **Act II — Mortality, from birth to resurrection (The Second Estate)**  
3. **Act III — Life After Death, Eternal Life**

He taught that we enter mortality like someone walking into a theater just as the curtain rises on Act II:

- We missed Act I  
- We don’t know all the relationships  
- We don’t see the full plot  
- We don’t know who is connected to whom  
- And we are not spectators — we are part of the cast  

## “Happily Ever After” Belongs to Act III

President Packer emphasized that the line:

> **“And they lived happily ever after”**

is **never** written into Act II.

That line belongs to Act III — when the mysteries are resolved, justice is restored, mercy is revealed, and everything is put right.

Without an eternal perspective, the inequities of life make little sense:

- some are born with so little  
- others with so much  
- some face poverty, pain, or disability  
- some suffer injustice  
- some die young  
- nature can be brutal  
- people can be brutal  

He taught:

> Do not suppose that God willfully causes what He permits for His own purposes.  
> When you understand the plan, even these things reveal a loving Father in Heaven.

## The Script of the Drama

The “script” of this three‑act play is found in the scriptures.  
Understanding the Plan of Salvation gives:

- purpose  
- direction  
- context  
- hope  

It explains why mortality feels confusing — because we are living in the middle of Act II without having seen Act I.

## The Villain in the Story

President Packer reminded us that there is a villain:

- the adversary  
- the schemer  
- the destroyer  

He rebelled in Act I and now seeks to spoil the plan for everyone.  
He has his own “cunning plan” — subtle, secret, and destructive.

## Our Place in the Drama

Here we are, on stage in Act II, living in the last days — a time of intense testing and unequaled opportunity.

Paul warned Timothy:

> “In the last days, perilous times shall come.” (2 Tim. 3:1)

President Packer taught that Paul’s description fits our day with remarkable accuracy.

Understanding the three‑act structure of the Plan helps us endure Act II with faith, patience, and perspective — knowing that Act III is where everything finally makes sense.
