# Thrust in your sickle

Thrust in your sickle

How much wheat was harvested by a sickle vs combine?  Approximately 1/2 acre per day can be harvested via a sickle vs 300 acres per day by a combine. Originally, an acre would feed about 12 people for a day.  Now, an acre (of Kansas wheat) can feed ~9,000 people per day on bread.  Or, put in a different light, in medieval times, they yielded about 5 bushels per acre of wheat.  The typical person requires about 24 bushels of wheat per year (one loaf of bread per day).  Thus, for a family of 10, they’d need about 48 acres, which would take 3 harvesters approximately a month of days to harvest.  Imagine gathering at that pace!  Now, today, not only are harvesters (combines) able to harvest about 300 acres per day, but an acre of Kansas wheat (95% is hard red wheat) can feed approximately 9000 people per day on bread.

There’s much to be learned from the Law of Moses with respect to caring for the poor, which was addressed by how much was to be left for gleaning. There is value in recognizing that they must still work to glean.
