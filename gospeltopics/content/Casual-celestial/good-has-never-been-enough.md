# Good Has Never Been Enough

**Mosiah 24:5 — “And they were a people friendly one with another; nevertheless, they knew not God.”**

We often assume that being “good people” is the goal — that kindness, friendliness, and general decency are the markers of discipleship. But the scriptures teach a deeper truth: *good has never been enough.* The Lord invites us not merely to be pleasant or moral, but to **know Him** and understand His dealings with His children.

To know God is to enter into covenant with Him — to accept not only His blessings but His laws. Anciently, Israel lived the **law of Moses**, a preparatory system pointing them toward Christ. Today, disciples live the **law of the gospel**, which includes the laws of **obedience**, **sacrifice**, and ultimately **consecration**. These laws are not burdens; they are pathways to transformation. They move us beyond being “good people” into becoming **covenant people**.

The people in Mosiah 24 were friendly and unified, yet they lacked the essential relationship that gives goodness its eternal weight. The Lord asks more of us because He intends to make more of us. Goodness is a beginning — but knowing God is the journey.
