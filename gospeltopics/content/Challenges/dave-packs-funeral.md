# Dave Pack’s Funeral

Below are the stories, moments, and experiences surrounding Dad’s funeral — the chaos, the emotion, the humor, and the heartbreak all woven together.

## Stories to Tell

- On the first day we arrived (2/2/23), Jenny and I were on our way to lunch when she realized John was at the house and needed a picture she had printed. We went inside to say hi, and pandemonium ensued. Kathryn was decompressing about caring for Dad, Liam was searching for Tiffany’s deaf emotional-support cat, Tiffany was melting down, and people were searching outside. I said, “I can’t look for a cat I don’t know what looks like.” Jenny was prompted to pray, and I spotted the cat almost immediately under the sofa.

- The Beethoven’s Symphony shower incident with John, Roger, Dad, and Kathryn (thank goodness we were at the Pho-King restaurant at the time).

- “Pure bloods only, but the muggle can pay for the funeral.” (Said while thanking us in tears.)

- I went to catalog Dad’s file cabinets. Kathryn was looking through his church bag. Tiffany and Liam came in and began going through things — Liam climbing into the top of Dad’s closet pulling out things he saved from Mom. Kathryn took Mom’s glasses. I was annoyed they were getting into Mom’s things. At that moment, John walked down the hall toward Dad’s bedroom to take a shower. Still not sure how he didn’t go full King Triton.

- I had to talk him down from that circus. He eventually calmed after everyone left except me and Jesse. Long debate about how he thinks the $1,000 rule works cumulatively. The next day Roger finds a $500+ Pearl of Great Price and says, “Hey! It’s under $1,000!”

- He offered to pay me $500 for the estate to come back and go through things. I told him he didn’t have to. He insisted. I told him to consider it the money he was going to count against me for books.

- He was relieved after we agreed to delay the 1–2–3–4 sibling sort.

- The next day, I asked to photograph Dad’s papers for family history. He said he understood but had “other perspectives to consider.” He talked about pressure from “other people” (still unclear who). He gave a hard no. I screamed at him — that I was trying to fulfill Dad’s dying wish. He said, “Get it all out. Go ahead and scream.” I brought up the pureblood rule. He denied saying it, then half‑acknowledged maybe he did. He accused me of “defcon 5 threats.”

- Funeral must be Friday per Father John, even though no one needed to travel. I missed a doctor appointment I’d waited 5 months for and a dental crown appointment that turned into a root canal.

- The day after Dad died, Kathryn got a scam call from “Comcast” and gave out Dad’s checking and routing numbers. John went to the bank but couldn’t stop payment without a death certificate. They moved all funds to savings.

- “Six will be enough for pallbearers… small people…” John and Roger would “rush to the rescue” even though Roger can’t lift 25 lbs and John walks with a cane.

- The obituary: Jenny wrote a draft. John insisted we say Dad supported the family financially. Jenny refused. Roger helped find language. Over-the-top career description followed by “David was a quiet, humble man.” Seven paragraphs long, sent two days late.

- Jenny begged to take Dad’s temple clothes to the funeral home — good thing she did. Dad had them in different places.

- Kathryn and John debated what to do with Dad’s garments. Jenny told them to give them to the bishop. They found one last pair and threw them in the trash.

- John removed Dad’s adult diaper to put on his garments. Emotional. Sent clean garments with me to the funeral home.

- Long discussion before waving off the 21‑gun salute.

- We wanted a flag on the coffin. Couldn’t find military records. Planned to buy a 3×5 flag. Kathryn steamed it in the bathroom with the end dragging on the floor. Jesse binder‑clipped it to keep it off the ground. Debate at the church about whether Kathryn pressed it well enough. John and Sue wanted to take it home to starch it. Kathryn offended. Brothers practiced folding it in the garage. John became emotional and dropped the flag on the filthy floor. Funeral director ended up handling it.

- “Superior intellect… can’t keep up with Jenny… Jenny’s timetable is not the only one.”

- Tiffany learned of Grandpa’s death right after a performance‑warning meeting at work. Couldn’t get time off. Threatened to quit. Went to Vegas the day after the funeral.

- Kathryn’s disability interview was the afternoon after she couldn’t find the braids and SF newspaper — the two things that meant the most to her from Mom.

- Bishop interactions: “Can you just sit on the stand to preside and John will emcee?”

- Tiffany bartering armchairs and the hope chest as if they were hers.

- The circus in Grandpa’s room when John walked in — led to buying a lock.

- “Only siblings allowed in the inner sanctum.”

- Grave dedication drama — Brandon couldn’t find his recommend.

- Kathryn sneaking things away.

- One usher: Logan (great‑grandson with Asperger’s, no experience). Never used.

- Jenny went nuclear — defcon 5 — and everyone finally agreed she should have access to the family history materials Dad wanted her to work on.

- Atreus downloading all the drama to Megan.

- Funeral setup went well because Jenny thought to bring pictures. It turned out beautifully.

## The Funeral Day

- The ward was gracious and supportive.
- Kathryn gave a nice talk.
- Roger did well and quoted Chief Tecumseh.
- Jenny’s talk was filled with the Spirit and ended with Dad’s testimony written in his scriptures on Dec 30, 2022.
- John… there are no words. Claimed he saw Dad at the funeral. Argued with the bishop about where he was sitting. Repeated “I stand on the shoulders of a great man” eight times. Declared “I am no longer a son.” Quoted the charity chapter, including irrelevant verses.

- Liam tried to keep Grandpa’s ring after putting it on at the casket.

## Graveside

- Pallbearers struggled but did not drop the casket.
- Brandon gave a beautiful dedication.
