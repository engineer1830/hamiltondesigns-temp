# Law of Moses

How does the Law of Moses point to Christ?

The Law of Moses was never meant to be an end in itself. It was a schoolmaster, a tutor, a symbolic system designed to prepare Israel to recognize, receive, and follow the Messiah. Every ordinance, every sacrifice, every ritual gesture pointed beyond itself to Christ.

## What Is the Law of Moses?

The Law of Moses was a covenantal system given to ancient Israel that combined:

- **Moral law** — commandments governing righteousness  
- **Ceremonial law** — sacrifices, offerings, festivals, purification  
- **Civil law** — community order, restitution, justice  

Its purpose was not merely to regulate behavior but to **teach Christ through symbols**. The shedding of blood, the role of the high priest, the Passover lamb, the Day of Atonement — all were prophetic shadows of the Savior’s mission.

## What Was the Basis of the Law?

The foundation of the Law of Moses was:

- **Obedience** — teaching Israel to submit their will to God  
- **Remembrance** — constant rituals to keep Christ’s future atonement in their minds  
- **Covenant belonging** — shaping Israel into a people prepared to receive the Messiah  
- **Symbolism** — every sacrifice testified of the One who would come to redeem them  

The law was never meant to save. It was meant to **point**.

## How Was It Changed Such That the Jews Did Not Respond to Jesus?

The tragedy of Israel’s rejection of Christ was not due to ignorance — they *recognized* Him. The scriptures record that they knew His works, His authority, and His fulfillment of prophecy.

So why did they not acknowledge Him?

- **The law became an end instead of a means.**  
  What was meant to point to Christ became, over generations, a system of self‑justification.

- **Ordinances replaced meaning.**  
  The symbols remained, but the symbolism was lost.

- **Tradition overshadowed revelation.**  
  The Pharisaic expansions of the law created a hedge so thick that the original purpose was obscured.

- **The Messiah they expected was not the Messiah who came.**  
  They looked for political deliverance, not spiritual redemption.

- **Pride hardened hearts.**  
  Those who held religious authority feared losing their influence.

The result:  
They saw Him.  
They recognized Him.  
But they would not receive Him.

The Law of Moses had done its job — it had pointed to Christ.  
But the people had stopped looking in the direction it pointed.
