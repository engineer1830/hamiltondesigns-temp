# Discipleship is Coming Unto Christ: Faith vs Works — Where does Grace Fit? - web

We choose faith and perform works as a result of our faith.

We must recognize that while we are busy working out our salvation, we are unable to bridge the gap with works . . . no matter how extraordinary we think our works are.

One must come to the realization that we have an Absolute need to rely upon our Savior. Coming unto Christ means giving whole souled devotion as we travel our journey of discipleship. It is the surrender of our Will. As we mature in our discipleship, we ultimately come to recognize that our Will is the only thing that we have to offer our Heavenly Father. There comes a point in our discipleship, often through our lived experiences, where we realize that we not only are NOT in control of the events of our life, but that we have NEVER been in control of the events of our life.

Insert brain tumor experience here.

What it all comes down to is that Coming Unto Christ means aligning our Will to the Will of our Father in Heaven. It is choosing to exercise faith that He knows what is best for us. Nevertheless, we prayerfully choose directions and work so that we are anxiously engaged in good causes and directions and we pursue our dreams all the while seeking guidance and trusting that we may recognize the hand of God in our lives. During the times of “plenty” in our lives is the time that we add the oil to our lamps that we may be prepared for the times that we must light those lamps and rely solely upon what oil we have accumulated through our lifetime of submitting our will to His. When the difficulties and trials of life are encountered, we continue forward and pray for understanding and we rely upon the “oil in our lamps” that we may endure until “joy cometh in the morning” and we trust that it will.

However, there are blessings and lessons that are learned in the crucible of life that cannot be learned any other way. The Lord has provided some of the most choice blessings while in those extremities. For me personally, it is the extremity of those challenges that have led to the most lasting testimony and understanding that I’ve learned. I have come to value to great capacity and intelligence of my wife and the magnitude of the gift and blessing she is to me ONLY because of the challenges that have come from walking through these extremities hand in hand with her. When you must walk into the shadow of the valley of death, it changes you. As in Alma 62:41 where some Nephites were hardened by the length of the war and some were softened, we have a choice in how we respond. By choosing faith . . . to allow ourselves to be softened by the trials of life such that we turn to our Heavenly Father and Savior Jesus Christ, we become a disciple rather than merely an admirer.

Martin Handcart story related by David O McKay (David O. McKay, "Pioneer Women," Relief Society Magazine, Jan. 1948, 8)

For each of us to come to know our Savior as disciples, we will each find him in our extremities, for that is when our hearts are most humbled and we are softened enough to finally align our will with His will. When we become as Peter and ask “Lord, to whom shall we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life.” John 6:68

President James E. Faust taught: (James E. Faust, "The Refiner's Fire'" Ensign, May 1979)

In the pain, the agony, and the heroic endeavors of life, we pass through a refiner's fire, and the insignificant and the unimportant in our lives can melt away like dross and make our faith bright, intact, and strong. In this way the divine image can be mirrored from the soul. It is part of the purging toll exacted of some to become acquainted with God. In the agonies of life, we seem to listen better to the faint, godly whisperings of the Divine Shepherd.

Turn to our Heavenly Father. President Jeffrey R. Holland taught: (Jeffrey R. Holland, "Lessons from Liberty Jail," Ensign, Sept. 2009, 26-33)

...every experience can become a redemptive experience if we remain bonded to our Father in Heaven through it. These difficult lessons teach us that man's extremity is God's opportunity, and if we will be humble and faithful, if we will be believing and not curse God for our problems, He can turn the unfair and inhumane and debilitating prisons of our lives into temples--or at least into a circumstance that can bring comfort and revelation, divine companionship and peace.

What do we do?
* Come Unto Christ
* Be Obedient — Make and Keep Covenants
* Serve God by serving our Fellow Men

Trust that God is capable of giving “beauty for ashes”. There are so many scenarios in which we may find our extremities. For some it is health; others it is the crush of injustice; for others it is betrayal by those closest to us . . . regardless of how we arrive, we will all, nonetheless arrive at a point where we are in our extremities. Don’t waste that opportunity to become acquainted with your Savior and Heavenly Father. When you find yourself at the point that there is only ONE source to turn, turn to HIM and find the peace that HE offers. Our living prophet Russell M. Nelson has taught us “the joy we feel has little to do with the circumstances of our lives and everything to do with the focus of our lives.” Keep his teaching in the forefront that we make remember to whom we look.
