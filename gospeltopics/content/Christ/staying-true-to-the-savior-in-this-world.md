Despair, or folly?  It is not despair, for despair is only for those who see the end beyond all doubt. We do not. Great insight from 1 Nephi 17:41 (https://bookofmormonstudynotes.blog/2017/07/28/because-of-the-simpleness-of-the-way-1-nephi-1741/). The lesson for us is the small and simple things:  Elder L. Whitney Clayton said:

Over time, that young bishop and I have observed that those who are deliberate about doing the “small and simple things”—obeying in seemingly little ways—are blessed with faith and strength that go far beyond the actual acts of obedience themselves and, in fact, may seem totally unrelated to them. It may seem hard to draw a connection between the basic daily acts of obedience and solutions to the big, complicated problems we face. But they are related. In my experience, getting the little daily habits of faith right is the single best way to fortify ourselves against the troubles of life, whatever they may be. Small acts of faith, even when they seem insignificant or entirely disconnected from the specific problems that vex us, bless us in all we do (“Whatsoever He Saith Unto You, Do It,” General Conference, April 2017).

President Packer said “True doctrine, understood, changes attitudes and behavior. The study of the doctrines of the gospel will improve behavior quicker than a study of behavior will improve behavior. Preoccupation with unworthy behavior can lead to unworthy behavior. That is why we stress so forcefully the study of the doctrines of the gospel.”

The study of the doctrines of the Gospel of Jesus Christ is a lifetime pursuit.  Progress and trajectory matter much more that perfectness.  Worthiness is not flawlessness.

* His father turned from righteousness and from the holy commandments

So, what did Abraham do?
* He was obedient (Abraham 1:1-2)
* He was a follower of righteousness
    * Things that Abraham desired:
        * Sought for the blessings of the fathers (the patriarchs such as Adam, Abel, Seth, Enoch, Noah, etc.)
        * To possess great knowledge and a greater knowledge
        * To be a greater follower of righteousness
        * To be a father of many nations
        * To be a prince of peace
        * Desiring to receive instructions and keep the commandments of God
    * He became . . .
        * A rightful heir
        * A High Priest
        * Appointed unto the Priesthood
* He left those who would do him harm

Do you think Abraham just accidentally was this way?  D&C 4 — he had desire!  He sought these things.  Compare to the words of President Nelson since he became the prophet.
