# There are No Guarantees

There is no guarantee . . . no money back warranty for this life.

Mosiah 23:21

One need only look to those who have lived righteously . . . even the one who lived perfectly. Their lives were not spent in luxury or bliss. Joseph and Emma lost 6 of their 11 children in infancy or before the age of 14 months. Neal A. Maxwell died of Leukemia, Gordon B. Hinckley had cancer, Russell M Nelson and Dallin H Oaks lost their wives with many years yet to serve and there are so many unnamed faithful members who serve and suffer silently and remain faithful regardless of the trial and challenges that come.

It is the point of life . . . I don’t understand the why in the eternal law that governs all things, but it seems to be unmistakably written that there is no other way that we may become as our Father without learning to endure our own sorrows, our own injustices, our own set backs, our own failures . . . and it is also written that there is and must be an Atonement to bridge the unavoidable gulf that separates us from our Father as we sojourn on this earth and endeavor to learn to love when it is the hardest to love and to trust when it is the darkest.

It seems as though we progress from challenge to challenge and trial to trial with each dependent upon the lessons offered in the previous trial. What once was impossible and unbearable becomes bearable . . . not that we ourselves become stronger, but that our faith and our trust becomes more sure. Until a man must step into the dark and learn for himself that there is “No other name by which . . .”, faith and trust struggle. The first time that you face that place that no one can go for you is the first time that you learn to put your hand in the hand of the Father.

Mosiah 23:22 — Trust and you shall be lifted up at the last day

Be careful about what we define as success and how we define success

Elder Jeffrey R Holland:

I often think of those of you who are in the midst of a struggle. As much as we want life to be easy and comfortable, as much as I wish it could be that way for you, it simply cannot be. We are all, in one way or another, at one point in our lives, going to deal with a moral conundrum or a difficult issue without an easy answer.  
At that point, we need to ask ourselves, “How much does the gospel of Jesus Christ really mean to me?” How will you act when that call comes? Will you defend Christ and His gospel, come what may?

John Taylor wrote that he once heard Joseph Smith say to the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, “You will have all kinds of trials to pass through. … God will feel after you, and He will take hold of you and wrench your very heart strings, and if you cannot stand it you will not be fit for an inheritance in the Celestial Kingdom of God.”  
The life of Christ was like that. It is not coincidental that the word that is used for Christ’s experience in Gethsemane is that He was in “agony.” If we say we’re disciples of Christ, we will on occasion be in agony. We must walk where He walked.

When those moments come—contemporary issues, historical complexities, personal problems at home, challenges in a mission or a marriage, wherever it is—I pray and ask and bless you to the end that you will be strong. May you follow Christ with every ounce of your being, in good times and in bad.
