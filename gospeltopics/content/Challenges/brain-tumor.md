# Brain Tumor

Things I’ve Learned Since the Brain Tumor

As Lamoni’s father said, “I will give away all my sins to know Thee” (Alma 22:18), I have been learning that I will give away all my worries to trust Thee.

The components of Dad:

Dad grew up knowing loss (father died when he was not even 2.5 years and step-father died when he was 15). About 15 years ago, I commented that I am what I have always been . . . based on that, you must have been one serious child, to which he replied “I am” in an austere voice!

Dad as a priesthood holder. Many of you called him President, but his final calling as Patriarch is much more true to how I knew him all my life. I speak of Father/Husband. The callings that changed him were as Father/Husband, temple worker, and patriarch.

Dad (and mom) as examples of enduring to the end. In watching and learning from them over the past decades is the true embodiment of what I envision with that element of the Doctrine of Christ, which is Faith in Jesus Christ, Repent and Obey, Ordinances (that begin with baptism and those available to us here on earth are completed in the temple), Receive the Holy Ghost (President Nelson’s counsel), and Enduring to the End, which means we build our faith through our actions and continue to repent and renew our ordinances (sacrament and returning to the temple on behalf of others), seeking and following counsel, and continuing until we return home to our Father.

Returning to priesthood — D&C 121:36-46

As a chemical engineer, distill is a verb that has unique meaning as I’ve studied the physical properties that are exploited through distillation. In essence, it is through heat (the refiner’s fire) that things are purified . . . drop by drop as the impurities of the “natural man” are left behind. Thus, the doctrines of the priesthood are gained over time, through that process of enduring to the end as we fortify ourselves in the Doctrine of Christ.

That is what I have learned from my father and my mother.

In 2020, we knew my father was sick and would most likely leave us before the year was up. On April 26, 2020, I woke up having had a dream that mom and dad and I were standing on the beach and dad gave me a hug after saying goodbye and turned and ran into the sun as mom and I stood together watching. He did not walk . . . he ran into the sun . . . not the sunset. I don’t have an ability to even imagine the joy that awaits us as we return to our Heavenly Father. For me, it will include a return to my father as well. It was this dream that reminded me that things here are not permanent and times come to a close.

President Eyring said: (https://www2.byui.edu/Speeches/eyring_jan2005.htm)

But if we have to be in trouble to want the Holy Ghost as a constant companion, then to have that steady desire we will have to be in steady trouble. There has to be a better way.  
Happily, there is. Now you will have to find your own. I’ll tell you mine. There is one for me that works: I choose to remind myself about my experience with what prophets have said about the peace and happiness that comes with the visitation of the Holy Ghost. It has been true in my life.  
. . .

I like to feel of that light and I like to be happy. I don’t have to wait for troubles and tests to make me want the help of the Holy Ghost. I can choose to remember what that companionship has been like, and whenever I do, I want that blessing again with my whole heart.
