# Parenting Challenges

My great grandmother (Flossie Wood) used to say:

> “When children are little, they step on your toes, and when they are big, they step on your heart.”

She was an incredibly sweet lady who I remember with fondness and smiles. Born in 1899 and passing in 1987, she lived nearly 50 years as a widow. My great grandfather died the year my father was born. She was short (4'9") and my favorite because as a child, I always felt safe with her.

## When Children Are Young

When our children are young, parenting is more straightforward — not easy, but clear. It is appropriate for us to provide direction and protection. We teach them:

- not to play in the street  
- not to touch a hot stove  
- not to run with scissors  
- to be respectful and kind  
- to eat their vegetables  

Our stewardship is hands-on, immediate, and directive.

## When They Grow

As they grow and venture out on their own, our role changes. We will always love them, but it is no longer our responsibility to “save” them from the consequences of their choices.

This shift becomes a sacred opportunity:

- to learn what it must be like for Heavenly Father  
- to watch those we love struggle  
- to pray for them  
- to love them despite mistakes  
- to trust the Atonement of Jesus Christ in a deeper way  

As we learn this kind of patient, enduring love, we begin to understand the wisdom and long-suffering of our Heavenly Father.
