# I Knew, Yet I Would Not Know
### Alma 10:5–6

Amulek’s confession is painfully honest: he *knew*, yet he “would not know.” He recognized truth, felt the Spirit, and understood God’s will — but he chose not to act. His words expose a spiritual pattern that is far more common than we like to admit.

How many times am I like this, even though I am “active”?  
How often do I live beneath my privilege simply because I am not more focused on the things of God?

Elder Clayton taught a powerful principle in *The Finest Homes*. He recounted asking stake presidents a simple question:

> Of the members who pay a full tithing, give a generous fast offering, magnify their callings, visit their families, hold family home evening, study the scriptures, and pray daily — how many have problems they cannot address on their own?

Their answer: **none**.

Why?  
Because they lived in “the finest homes.” Their faithful living brought strength, vision, and heavenly help — not immunity from trials, but divine capacity to move through them with peace and hope.

President Nelson has pled with us to increase our spiritual capacity to receive revelation. He warned that in coming days, it will not be possible to survive spiritually without the constant influence of the Holy Ghost.

The strength we need is available to all of us.  
The question is whether we will *know* — or whether we will, like Amulek, choose not to know.

This is the invitation:  
To stop living beneath our privilege and begin living with spiritual intention, spiritual discipline, and spiritual hunger.
